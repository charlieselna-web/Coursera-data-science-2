Starting with context

What data science leaders at learning platforms tend to be working on.

Based on publicly available signals and what we hear from similar roles, here are three themes that often sit at the center of a data science leader's agenda at a platform like Coursera. These may or may not reflect your exact priorities — but they shaped how we thought about where Glean might be relevant.

🎯

Personalize the learning journey end-to-end

Better recommendations, smarter nudges, and content sequencing that actually improves activation, completion, and NRR.

🤖

Scale AI across the learner & educator experience

AI-assisted authoring, grading, and skills tagging that improves content supply, learner outcomes, and operating leverage.

🏗️

Unify metrics, experimentation, and data infrastructure

Standardize KPI definitions, accelerate the Databricks migration, and give every team a single trusted foundation — so decisions move faster and debates happen less often.

Common friction points

Where these priorities tend to get complicated.

These aren't meant to be prescriptive — every org is different. But across data science teams we talk to, a few challenges tend to surface consistently as the ambitions above scale up.

📉

"Are we moving the right metric?"

Optimizing for engagement or conversion without grounding in true learner outcomes or enterprise retention. A win in the experiment. A loss in the renewal.

⚠️

"Will our AI be trusted at scale?"

Inaccurate grading, unexplainable recommendations, or biased tagging erode trust faster than any product bug — with educators, learners, and leadership alike.

🔀

"Is the platform becoming the bottleneck?"

Messy pipelines, inconsistent metrics, and migration drag slow every team down. Even the best data scientists can't move fast on a fragmented foundation.

Where Glean fits

How teams like yours are using it.

Glean is an enterprise AI search and knowledge platform — it connects the fragmented context your team already has across tools (experiments, docs, code, decisions, tickets) in one permission-aware, auditable layer. Here's how that maps to the challenges above.

Risk

Teams optimize for the wrong metric because experiment rationale, KPI definitions, and past analyses are scattered across Confluence, Notion, Slack, and email.

→

Glean

Surfaces the full context behind any metric — prior experiments, assumptions, stakeholder decisions, and outcome signals — so teams don't repeat mistakes or chase local wins.

Risk

→

Glean

Grounds AI answers in governed internal knowledge with source traceability and permission controls — built to complement tools like Copilot or ChatGPT, not replace them.

Risk

Data migrations, platform standardization, and cross-functional alignment all stall when teams can't find owners, historical context, or decisions that were made months ago.

→

Glean

Connects code, docs, tickets, and decisions across every tool in one place — so your team spends less time hunting and more time building. Less rework. Fewer interruptions. Faster decisions.
